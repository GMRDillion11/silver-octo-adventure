Our Project

Community Transformation through Moral Restoration & Peacebuilding

Project Title

Community Transformation through Moral Restoration, Youth Empowerment, Victim’s Care, and Peacebuilding in Southern Nigeria

Executive Summary

Golden Rule Mandate in the African Nation (GR-MAN) seeks partnership and funding support to implement a multi-tiered peacebuilding initiative aimed at countering genocide, ethnic and tribal conflicts, and poverty-driven violence in Africa, beginning with Nigeria.

Rooted in the universal Golden Rule principle, this project leverages community education, interfaith dialogue, youth empowerment, and ethical leadership development to foster social cohesion and sustainable peace.

Background & Problem Statement

  • Ethno-religious conflicts across Nigeria
  • Farmer–herder clashes in the Middle Belt
  • Insurgent and extremist violence
  • Resource-based conflicts in the Niger Delta
  • High youth unemployment and poverty
  • Decline in moral and community values

Project Goal

To strengthen moral awareness, reduce conflict, and promote sustainable peace and development by empowering communities with ethical values, conflict-resolution tools, and economic resilience strategies.

Specific Objectives

  1. Promote moral reorientation using the Golden Rule.
  2. Prevent ethnic and tribal violence through dialogue.
  3. Empower youth and vulnerable populations economically.
  4. Strengthen interfaith collaboration for peace.
  5. Build local capacity in peace education and mediation.
  6. Establish Peace and Values Clubs in communities.

Proposed Activities

A. Moral Reorientation

  • Golden Rule curriculum development
  • Community ethics seminars
  • School and faith-based workshops

B. Interfaith Dialogue

  • Multi-Stakeholder Peace Councils
  • Quarterly mediation forums
  • Peace walks and unity campaigns

C. Youth Empowerment

  • Vocational skills training
  • Entrepreneurship development
  • Youth cooperatives and micro-grants

D. Conflict Prevention

  • Early-warning systems
  • Community peace monitors
  • Rapid-response mediation teams

Target Beneficiaries

  • Youth (15–35 years)
  • Women and widows affected by conflict
  • Religious and traditional leaders
  • Ethnic minority groups
  • Schools and community mentors

Geographic Focus (Pilot Phase)

  • Kaduna State
  • Plateau State
  • Benue State
  • Adamawa & Borno States
  • Niger Delta Communities
  • Federal Capital Territory (FCT)

Conclusion

GR-MAN is committed to building a peaceful, morally grounded, and resilient Africa. Through this project, communities will gain tools for non-violence, ethical leadership, and sustainable development.
